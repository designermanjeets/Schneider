General Safety Instructions

This document assumes that the Conext Gateway inverter has never been energized and that external disconnecting means have been provided to lock-out and tag-out all power sources.

danger

MULTIPLE SOURCES WITH HAZARD OF ELECTRIC SHOCK, EXPLOSION, ARC FLASH, AND FIRE

  • This equipment must be installed only by qualified personnel and serviced only by authorized service personnel equipped with appropriate personal protective equipment and following safe electrical work practices.
  • This equipment is for use in closed electrical operating (restricted access) areas only.
  • Before opening any doors or covers (except for the LV and Communications section door and the Communications Wiring section door):
    • Consult the system diagram to identify all power sources. This equipment is energized from multiple sources: the DC input, the AC grid, and the auxiliary AC source. When the PV array is exposed to light, it supplies a DC voltage to this equipment.
    • De-energize, lock out, and tag out all power sources.
    • Wait at least (Undefined variable: SE1.Discharge Wait Time) minutes for internal capacitors to discharge to safe voltages.
    • Wearing appropriate personal protective equipment, verify that all circuits are de-energized using a suitably rated meter.
  • Additional external disconnecting means for the DC, AC, and auxiliary AC source, capable of being locked-out and tagged-out, must be provided as part of the installation. Operation of switches in or on this equipment does not remove all power from this equipment.
  • Reinstall or close all covers and doors before energizing this equipment.
  • Do not defeat any of the door interlocks.
  • Allow liquid coolant to cool before servicing.

Failure to follow these instructions will result in death or serious injury.

danger

hazard of crush injury

  • Use caution and follow the instructions in Related Documents for correct lifting, moving, and mounting of the Conext Gateway shipping crate and inverter.
  • Do not lift or move the shipping crate or the inverter by other than the recommended lifting points and methods. The inverter weighs approximately (Undefined variable: SE1.Inverter weight) including the packing crate and pallet.
  • Use appropriately-rated lifting equipment. Lifting equipment must be operated by a trained and qualified operator.
  • Do not put any part of your body underneath the raised pallet, including your hands.
  • Do not lift the inverter more than 203 mm (8 in.) with a forklift.
  • Securely attach the inverter to a level mounting surface after positioning to prevent the inverter from toppling.
  • Keep all the doors closed and latched when moving the inverter.

Failure to follow these instructions will result in death or serious injury.

WARNING

Hazard of electric shock, explosion, arc flash, and fire

  • Ensure the floor plates are installed and are sealed. Ensure the area around each cable and conduit is sealed.
  • Materials and workmanship must meet and maintain the requirements for IP65 or Type 4X enclosure standards, depending on local codes and standards.

Failure to follow these instructions can result in death, serious injury, or equipment damage.

NOTE: Failure to properly install and seal the floor plates may result in equipment damage that is not covered by your warranty.

WARNING

Hazard of electric shock, explosion, arc flash, and fire

  • Do not open any inverter doors or covers when the environmental conditions indicate a possibility of rain/moisture, strong wind, blowing dust, or other contamination entering the enclosure.
  • To reduce the risk of vermin ingress, which can result in damage or contamination, do not leave the inverter unattended when the inverter doors are open.
  • If the service area is not dry and free of debris, all work must be postponed until the environmental conditions have cleared, or the CompanyName–approved service tent has been installed and the debris has been removed.
  • The acceptable minimum outdoor temperature for servicing the Conext Gateway inverter is ‑25°C (‑13°F). If the outdoor temperature is outside this limit, postpone all work until the temperature is within the acceptable limit.

Failure to follow these instructions can result in death, serious injury, or equipment damage.

WARNING

hazard of electric shock, explosion, arc flash, and fire

  • Connect the AC output and/or input of the inverter to a suitably rated isolating transformer, with separate primary and secondary windings.

  • Select the correct isolation transformer according to the Medium Voltage Transformer Requirements Application Note (document number AP-SG-008) and Related Documents.

Failure to follow these instructions can result in death, serious injury, or equipment damage.
